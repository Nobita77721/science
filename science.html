Famous Scientists

Isaac Newton Albert Einstein Marie Curie Charles Darwin Michael Faraday Nikola Tesla Alan Turing Werner Heisenberg Stephen Hawking Emmy Noether

Isaac Newton

Born: January 4, 1643
Died: March 31, 1727
Achievements: Laws of motion, theory of gravity, calculus.

Albert Einstein

Born: March 14, 1879
Died: April 18, 1955
Achievements: Theory of relativity, photoelectric effect.

Marie Curie

Born: November 7, 1867
Died: July 4, 1934
Achievements: Radioactivity, Nobel Prizes in Physics and Chemistry.

Charles Darwin

Born: February 12, 1809
Died: April 19, 1882
Achievements: Theory of evolution by natural selection.

Michael Faraday

Born: September 22, 1791
Died: August 25, 1867
Achievements: Electromagnetism, electrochemistry.

Nikola Tesla

Born: July 10, 1856
Died: January 7, 1943
Achievements: AC electricity, Tesla coil.

Alan Turing

Born: June 23, 1912
Died: June 7, 1954
Achievements: Father of computer science, Turing machine.

Werner Heisenberg

Born: December 5, 1901
Died: February 1, 1976
Achievements: Quantum mechanics, Uncertainty principle.

Stephen Hawking

Born: January 8, 1942
Died: March 14, 2018
Achievements: Black holes, Hawking radiation.

Emmy Noether

Born: March 23, 1882
Died: April 14, 1935
Achievements: Noether's theorem, abstract algebra.

Physics Overview

Physics is the study of matter, energy, and the fundamental forces of nature.

Chemistry Overview

Chemistry is the study of substances, their properties, and how they interact with one another.

Biology Overview

Biology is the study of living organisms, their structure, function, growth, and evolution.

Astronomy Overview

Astronomy is the study of celestial objects, space, and the universe as a whole.

Geology Overview

Geology is the study of the Earth, its processes, materials, and history.

Mathematics Overview

Mathematics is the study of numbers, quantities, shapes, and patterns.

Environmental Science Overview

Environmental Science is the study of the interactions between the physical, chemical, and biological components of the environment.
